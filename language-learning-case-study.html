Language Learning App — HCI Class Project

A user-centered semester project (team of 4) where we researched, prototyped, and tested a language-learning mobile app — from competitor analysis and interviews to interactive wireframes.

Role: UX Research & Design Tools: Figma · Miro · Google Forms · Google Drive Class: HCI

Project Overview

Full Research (PDF) Interviews (PDF)

Short description: We identified gaps in personalization and conversational practice in popular language apps, conducted interviews across user personas, created a story map and mood board, and built an interactive prototype in Figma.

Process

Research & Analysis

Field research & competitor analysis highlighted market opportunities and KPIs.

  • Competitor matrix & market sizing
  • Personas & job stories

User Interviews

We ran interviews and synthesized pain points: lack of personalization, limited speaking practice, and expensive subscriptions.

  • 5+ interview transcripts
  • Key insights & quotes

Design & Prototype

Using the insights we created mood boards, story maps, and high-fidelity wireframes. The interactive prototype is embedded below.

Key Outcomes

  • Defined MVP features: personalized lessons, speaking practice, note-taking, and progress tracking.
  • Documented positioning and market model (TAM/SAM/SOM).
  • Built a clickable prototype used for feedback and iteration.

Reflection & Next Steps

What I'd improve: add text/audio analysis to match users with tutors, implement offline mode, and run usability tests with 10+ participants.
